THE PROJECT

Art on a Scale You Can Compare to a Continent

The stage for the 2024 challenge was Peru, South America. Using a vast landscape ranging from sea level to altitudes of 4,000m as his canvas, Naoki set out to draw an "Alpaca" — roughly 3,000km (3,000,000m) in body length — as GPS art, on a scale that can be compared to an entire continent.

Over approximately 90 days, he traveled and drew across a total distance of 4,600km. Due to budget and safety constraints, he was only able to run about 1,000km of it on foot. The rest he covered by every means available — hitchhiking, shared minibuses and more — until at last the Alpaca geoglyph was complete.

The completed "Alpaca" geoglyph (GPS art)

THE JOURNEY

Through Fraud, Feral Dogs, and Getting Lost

He was defrauded of 3,000 dollars, attacked again and again by packs of feral dogs in the desert, and abandoned by a taxi in the mountains at 4,000m of altitude, where he nearly became lost for good. It was a journey so brutal that his toenails came off — yet, supported by a team of around 240 people cheering him on, he made it back to Japan alive.
