01 — PROJECT

About the Project

In 2026, the world's eyes turn to the Americas.

For the first time in history, the FIFA World Cup will be co-hosted by three nations: the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

With an audience of 3.5–5 billion viewers, Naoki will ride this global wave of attention to run through 11 countries and create GPS art that delivers excitement and smiles to the world.

The first leg spans the three host countries: USA, Mexico, and Canada. Naoki will draw all three official FIFA World Cup 2026 mascots at Guinness-scale sizes, fusing sport and art to celebrate the tournament.

The finish line is Manhattan, New York City! The goal is to arrive the day before the World Cup Opening Ceremony — then pivot south for the second leg across 8 South American countries.

After arriving in the US, Naoki will spend roughly 5 months making his way toward Brazil.

▶ First Leg — North & Central America
Create GPS art in 3 countries to celebrate the World Cup!

▶ Second Leg — South America
Using the World Cup Opening Ceremony as the starting gun, head south into soccer-mad South America. Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia, Chile, Argentina, Uruguay, Brazil — 8 countries of GPS art and adventure!

02 — ADVENTURE

An Adventure on the Edge

Naoki has already traveled through 30+ countries — getting scammed out of $3,000, nearly killed by a pack of wild dogs, and stranded at 4,000m in the Andes after being abandoned by a taxi driver.

This time he pushes even further — 11 countries through Central and South America, arguably the most challenging route yet. "Stay safe" is the top priority for this project. (Even a safe room to sleep in makes a difference.)

03 — WHY

Why He Keeps Going

He keeps going because no human being has ever done this before — and because there are emotions waiting on the other side that nobody has ever felt.

Art-guided alleys and landmark routes through unknown streets.

Running and drawing there, what kind of smiles and scenery will he encounter? He'll share every joy and discovery on Instagram and YouTube — inviting the world along for the ride.

This project has 10 team members so far. The 11th is you.

04 — NEXT GENERATION

A Legacy for the Next Generation

Naoki's journey began as an elementary school teacher, when spending two years as the homeroom teacher for a student with cerebral palsy planted a dream: "Create a running culture where nobody has to compete."

This challenge is also a message to his beloved former students and children everywhere — that the joy of daring to try is worth chasing, and that the connections we build across borders belong to the next generation.

Your support and encouragement becomes the fuel for the next generation of challengers.
